Correlation vs regression


Overview

Take note:

  1. Correlation:
    ‚ûä Association between two quantitative variables.
    ‚ûã Linear association; in which one variable increases/decreases for each increase/decrease in the other.
  2. Regression:

Regression equation

Take note:

  1. Correlation describes the strength of an association between two variables, and is completely symmetrical;
    ‚û† the correlation between A and B is the same as the correlation between B and A.
  2. If y = dependent variable, and x = independent variable, this relationship is described as the regression of y on x.

    ‚û† Regression simply means that y changes with x.
  3. Regression equation is often more useful than the correlation coefficient.

    üåü We can predict y from x.

    y = anatomical dead space of lung (ml)

    y = -82.4 + 1.033x
    ‚û† On average, for every increase in height of 1 cm, the increase in anatomical dead space is 1.033 ml over the range of measurements made.
  5. Multiple regression:
    ‚û† Predict dependent variable from multiple independent variables.
